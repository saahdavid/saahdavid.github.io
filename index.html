About

Scholar.
Educator.
Innovator.

I am a statistician and educator whose research centers on probability distribution theory, with a particular focus on flexible models for imprecise and uncertain data. My doctoral dissertation at the University of Nevada, Reno introduced a new generalization approach for statistical models with broad applications across science and engineering.

In the classroom, I prioritize clarity, curiosity, and active engagement — encouraging students to question assumptions and think independently. My teaching is shaped by my research, and I aim to show how abstract ideas translate into real-world practice.

What motivates me most is helping students develop the tools to think critically long after the course ends.

I am committed to fostering an inclusive and engaging learning environment that promotes active learning, critical thinking, and real-world application. My teaching approach integrates interactive problem-solving, hands-on data analysis, and collaborative group work to enhance student engagement. I emphasize a growth mindset — providing constructive feedback and support to help students develop resilience and confidence in mathematics and statistics. My goal is to inspire students to appreciate the relevance of mathematical concepts and equip them with problem-solving skills applicable beyond the classroom.
